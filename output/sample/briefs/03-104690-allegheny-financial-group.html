Suggested angle

Given that Allegheny already runs 13 private funds in-house across PE, hedge, and real estate, the conversation should not be about introducing alts conceptually — it should be about complementing or expanding what they already do. An evergreen vehicle discussion fits naturally here: the firm's 98% discretionary ratio and Fidelity custody relationship suggest they have the operational readiness to deploy a continuously offered, semi-liquid structure alongside their existing closed-end fund lineup, potentially solving for client liquidity preferences without abandoning private market exposure.
