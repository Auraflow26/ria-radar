Positioning

Mill Creek Capital Advisors is a Conshohocken, PA-based RIA managing $10.5B in total regulatory AUM, with an extraordinary concentration of discretionary control — $10.4B, or effectively 100% of assets, is managed on a discretionary basis. The firm serves 365 high-net-worth individual clients who collectively account for $7.2B (69%) of total AUM, signaling a deep-pocketed, relationship-intensive clientele. With 42 employees, the firm operates at a high AUM-per-employee scale characteristic of a tightly run, institutionally oriented boutique.

Why they're alts-ready

  • Already advises 4 private funds — 2 private equity and 1 hedge fund — with $754M in gross assets, confirming active, in-house alternatives management at institutional scale.
  • 100% discretionary AUM ($10.4B) means the investment team can act decisively on new product allocations without client-by-client approval friction.
  • $7.2B of AUM is attributed to 365 HNW individual clients, a profile that maps directly to the typical evergreen/interval fund investor universe.
  • Homepage explicitly features private markets, private equity, and private credit as vehicle structures, indicating alts are front-and-center in client-facing positioning — not an afterthought.

Current alternatives footprint

Per ADV Item 7.B, Mill Creek advises 4 private funds: 2 private equity funds and 1 hedge fund, with combined gross assets of $754M. The firm's homepage prominently references private markets, private equity, and private credit as core investment structures. This is a firm that is already deep in alternatives — both operationally (running proprietary funds) and in its client-facing narrative.

Suggested angle

Because Mill Creek already manages proprietary PE and hedge fund vehicles, the most credible entry point is not "introducing" alternatives — it's a conversation about how third-party evergreen or interval fund structures can complement or extend their existing private markets exposure for clients who may want diversification across managers or strategies they don't run in-house. With custody at both Charles Schwab (Schwab Alternative Investment Marketplace) and Fidelity (alternatives platform for advisors), the operational infrastructure to onboard additional alts products is already in place, removing a common friction point.

Conversation starters

  • You're already running $754M across 4 proprietary private funds — where do you find yourselves going outside the house for alternatives exposure, and what's driving those decisions?
  • With $7.2B in HNW client assets and 100% discretionary authority, how are you currently thinking about the balance between your own fund vehicles and third-party private markets products on your platform?
  • You're custodying at both Schwab and Fidelity, both of which operate dedicated alternatives marketplaces — are you actively using those platforms today, and is there anything about the current product shelf that isn't meeting your needs?
