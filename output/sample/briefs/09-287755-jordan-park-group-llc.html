The firm is deeply embedded in private markets, directly advising 45 private funds with $6.6B in gross assets — making alternatives not a peripheral offering but a core pillar of its investment practice.

Suggested angle

Given Jordan Park's existing $6.6B private fund complex and fully discretionary book serving 163 ultra-HNW clients, the most natural conversation is around evergreen or interval fund structures that could complement their existing illiquid sleeve — offering clients continuous-commitment optionality without the vintage-year concentration of traditional closed-end drawdown vehicles. With 100% discretionary authority over $23.3B and custody already established at both Schwab and Fidelity, the operational lift for adding a third-party evergreen vehicle is low and the portfolio fit conversation can stay at the strategy level, not the plumbing level.
