Positioning

Brave Warrior Advisors is a concentrated, high-conviction New York-based RIA managing $5.6B in total regulatory AUM — 100% discretionary — for a tight roster of 98 clients, 82 of whom are high-net-worth individuals accounting for $3.9B of that AUM. With only 6 employees overseeing $5.6B, this is a lean, institutionally scaled operation built for deep relationships and large per-client allocations.

Why they're alts-ready

  • Already runs a private-fund business: advises 4 hedge funds with $220M in gross assets (Item 7.B confirmed Yes).
  • HNW client concentration is extreme — $3.9B of the firm's $5.6B RAUM is attributed to just 82 HNW individuals, signaling large average relationships capable of meeting high minimums.
  • 100% of the firm's $5.6B RAUM is held on a discretionary basis, giving the PM full authority to allocate into new structures without client-by-client approval.
  • Custodian is Pershing (BNY), which operates the Alts Bridge platform — an existing operational pathway for alternative product subscriptions.

Suggested angle

Given that Brave Warrior already structures and manages its own hedge funds, the conversation should center on evergreen private credit or private equity vehicles as a complement — not a replacement — to what they build internally. The pitch is operational efficiency and diversification: rather than spinning up another fund structure, an evergreen vehicle on the Alts Bridge platform at Pershing lets them access a different risk/return profile for their 82 HNW clients with minimal back-office lift. Lead with the custodian overlap as the natural on-ramp.

Conversation starters

  • You're already running 4 hedge funds with $220M in gross assets internally — when you think about the next sleeve you'd want to add for your HNW clients, is it more of the same strategy or are you looking to diversify the alternatives exposure?
  • With $5.6B fully discretionary and only 6 employees, operational simplicity has to matter — how are you currently handling the subscription and reporting workflow for your private fund clients, and is there friction you'd want to reduce?
  • Your book sits at Pershing, which runs the Alts Bridge platform — have you had a chance to evaluate what's available there, or has your focus been primarily on the funds you manage directly?
